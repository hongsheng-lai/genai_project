Abstract
Since the seminal work by Meirowitz [12], there has been growing attention on the exis-
tence and uniqueness of continuous Bayesian Nash equilibria. In the existing literature, ex-
istence is typically established using Schauderâ€™s fixed-point theorem, relying on the equicon-
tinuity of playersâ€™ best response functions. Uniqueness, on the other hand, is usually derived
under additional monotonicity conditions. In this paper, we revisit the issues of existence
and uniqueness, and advance the literature by establishing both simultaneously using the
Banach fixed-point theorem under a set of moderate conditions. Furthermore, we analyze
the stability of such equilibria with respect to perturbations in the joint probability distri-
bution of type parameters, offering theoretical support for the application of Bayesian Nash
equilibrium models in data-driven contexts.
Keywords: Bayesian Nash equilibrium, contraction, existence and uniqueness, stability
analysis

Introduction
In recent years, there has been a growing focus on Nash games involving private information. A
common assumption in such games is that the initial distribution of all playersâ€™ types is publicly
known. Each player has full knowledge of their own type, which determines their utility func-
tion, while remaining unaware of their opponentsâ€™ types. Based on the prior information, each
player selects a response strategy within their type space based on the Nash conjecture, and an
equilibrium arising from this kind of game is known as a Bayesian Nash equilibrium (BNE). The
concept of the Bayesian game was introduced by Harsanyi [7, 8, 9] and has attracted consid-
erable attention, spanning from modeling to equilibrium analysis and applications in strategic
decision-making under incomplete information.

Existence of pure strategy Bayesian Nash equilibria (BNEs) has been studied extensively
ever since. A majority of research on the existence of monotone BNE (MBNE) has focused
on conditions related to supermodularity and single-crossing condition (SCC). Vives [23] ana-
lyzes existence of Nash equilibria of non-cooperative games by using lattice theoretical methods,
where each playerâ€™s payoffs satisfy certain supermodularity properties which are directly related
to strategic complementarities. The author also establishes existence of BNE as the supermod-
ularity is preserved by integration.
Athey [2] studied Bayesian games with one-dimensional
actions and type spaces, assuming that types are drawn from an atomless joint probability dis-
tribution. When each playerâ€™s expected utility function exhibits SCC property, i.e., each playerâ€™s
optimal response is increasing with respect to the increase of its type parameter in response to
rivalsâ€™ increasing strategies (for type varying), she demonstrated existence of pure strategy Nash
equilibria (PSNEs) in every finite action game with each playerâ€™s optimal response strategy being
increasing and step-like. Moreover, when action spaces are continuous, Athey shows existence of
a sequence of increasing step-like PSNEs of finite action games that converges to an equilibrium
with the continuum-action, which means that an equilibrium in continuous action spaces can be
approximated by a sequence of increasing step-like PSNEs in finite action spaces. Mcadams [11]
extends Atheyâ€™s results into more general settings by considering the quasi-supermodularity (in
own action) and single-crossing (in own action and type), where both action and type spaces
are considered to be multidimensional and only partially ordered. Reny [15] further relaxes the
requirements on payoff functions, showing that monotone best-reply conditions can hold under
weaker assumptions. Van Zandt and Vives [22] introduce a concept called â€œmonotone super-
modularityâ€ in Bayesian games, where, apart from strategic complementarities, each playerâ€™s
payoff also has increasing differences in their own action and the profile of types, and that each
playerâ€™s posterior beliefs are increasing in type with respect to first-order stochastic dominance.
They provide an induction method to construct a greatest and a least BNE, with both equilibria
being monotone in type.
Another research stream focuses on the continuity property of BNEs. By using Schauderâ€™s
fixed-point theorem under some moderate conditions, Meirowitz [12] establishes existence of
a continuous BNE (CBNE) with each playerâ€™s equilibrium strategy being continuous.
One
of the main conditions is the equicontinuity of the set of optimal response strategies, which
ensures that the operator mapping the set of optimal response strategies to itself is compact.
Meirowitz comments that the equicontinuity condition is either likely to fail or difficult to verify
in practical applications. Ui [21] provides a sufficient condition for existence and uniqueness of a
differentiable BNE by regarding it as a solution of a variational inequality problem. The author
demonstrates that when the Jacobian matrix of the payoff gradient is negative definite for each
state, a BNE is unique. Guo et al. [6] take a step forward by presenting some verifiable conditions
for the required equicontinuity, namely, some growth conditions of the expected utility function
of each player. Moreover, under some monotonicity conditions, the authors demonstrate the
uniqueness of a CBNE. The authors also develop some computational approaches for finding an
approximate CBNE by using the polynomial decision rule to restrict the strategy function.
More recently, there has been some research extending the BNE model to the generalized
Bayesian Nash equilibrium (GBNE) model, where the key difference between GBNE and BNE
is that, in GBNE, the action space depends on the type parameter or rivalsâ€™ actions due to

some constraints. Liu et al. [10] propose the price competition model under multinomial logit
demand where each firmâ€™s marginal cost is private information, and each firmâ€™s action space is
dependent on its marginal cost. Tao and Xu [18] consider a generalized Bayesian game where
each playerâ€™s action space depends on its own type parameter and the rivalsâ€™ actions. Under
some moderate conditions, they demonstrate existence of continuous GBNE and the uniqueness
of such an equilibrium when each playerâ€™s action space is only dependent on its type. In the case
that each playerâ€™s action space is also dependent on rivalsâ€™ actions, they give a simple example to
show that the uniqueness of GBNE is not guaranteed under standard monotone conditions. In a
further study, Su and Xu [17] introduce a Bayesian Nash risk equilibrium (BNRE) model, where
they considered the concepts of aleatoric uncertainty and epistemic uncertainty, discussing the
model from two perspectives: monotonicity of a continuous BNRE and continuity of a monotone
BNRE.
In this paper, we revisit the BNE problem with two new and substantial theoretical contri-
butions.
â€¢ We establish the Lipschitz continuity of each playerâ€™s optimal response function with re-
spect to both their own type parameter and the actions of their rivals, by leveraging the
classical implicit function theorem rather than relying on growth conditions under the
assumption that each playerâ€™s utility function is strongly monotone (Theorem 3.1). This
result enables us to simultaneously establish the existence and uniqueness of CBNE using
the Banach fixed-point theorem (Theorem 3.2).
â€¢ Under a set of moderate conditions, we quantify the stability and sensitivity of the CBNE
with respect to perturbations in the joint probability distribution of type parameters (The-
orems 4.1 and 4.2).
These new results address an important gap in the literature on
Bayesian Nash equilibria and provide theoretical foundations for applying BNE models
to data-driven problems, where the true distribution of type parameters is often only
approximately recovered from empirical data.

Existence and uniqueness
In this section, we investigate the existence and uniqueness of a CMBNE. In the literature of
Bayesian games, there are two primary approaches to establishing the existence of a BNE, de-
pending on the structure of the objective functions. One is based on Schauderâ€™s fixed-point theo-
rem, see e.g. [6, 12, 18], where the BNEs are continuous with respect to type varying. The other
is based on the fixed-point theorems associated with single-crossing and quasi-supermodularity
properties, see e.g. [2, 11, 15, 22, 23], where the BNEs are not necessarily continuous but mono-
tone under some moderate conditions. Su and Xu [17] examined the existence of BNRE models
from two perspectives: the monotonicity of a continuous BNRE and the continuity of a mono-
tone BNRE, thereby unifying two strands of existing research. In this section, we revisit this
issue by presenting a novel approach for establishing existence and uniqueness. Specifically, we
focus on the case where each playerâ€™s objective function is strongly monotone in its action and
uniformly so with respect to other parameters.

Concluding Remarks
In this paper, we explore a new way to derive existence and uniqueness of continuous Bayesian
Nash equilibrium and we have managed to do so by deriving Lipschitz continuity of each playerâ€™s
optimal response function w.r.t. variation of its type parameter and the rivalsâ€™ optimal response
functions uniformly w.r.t. other parameters. We derive a contraction condition, we enables us to
derive the existence and uniqueness of the equilibrium by virtue of Banach fixed-point theorem
which significantly simply the proofs in the literature [6, 21]. We also make the first attempt to
quantify impact of perturbation of the joint probability distribution of type parameters on the
CBNE by deriving an explicit error bound. The new results will have potential effect on deriving
new computational methods for solving an approximate CBNE. It remains an open question as
to whether similar results can be established where the space of type parameters is unbounded.
